# HelloTalk Visual Style Guide v2

Brand visual identity for blog images. Reference when crafting prompts to ensure consistency across articles.

---

## Brand Personality

HelloTalk blog visuals should feel:

- **Vibrant and optimistic** — airy atmosphere, bright studio lighting, friendly energy
- **Friendly but credible** — like a knowledgeable friend, not a pushy salesperson
- **Modern and clean** — photorealistic website/app marketing compositions with bold purple as the anchor color
- **Visually rich, not sparse** — every image should have a decorative geometry layer, filled card backgrounds, and strong brand presence. White space is intentional breathing room, not emptiness.
- **Multicultural without stereotypes** — use generated, non-identifiable people and avoid flag clichés, globe icons, and generic stock-photo tropes

## Color Usage Rules

### Dominant color principle

HelloTalk Purple `#5856D6` should be **the single dominant color** in every infographic. It anchors the brand identity instantly.

### Accent ratio

In a typical infographic:
- **60-70%** white or soft lavender background (airy feeling)
- **20-25%** HelloTalk Purple (charts, headers, key data points)
- **5-15%** bright accents (green / pink / yellow), used sparingly for emphasis

### What NOT to do with color

- ❌ Don't use all three accent colors at full saturation together (looks cluttered)
- ❌ Don't replace HelloTalk Purple with similar-looking purples (it must be `#5856D6`)
- ❌ Don't use the old palette (HelloTalk Blue `#4A90E2`, coral `#FF7A6B`) — that's outdated
- ❌ Don't use saturated neon colors outside the accent palette

## v2 Visual Richness Layer

This is the primary upgrade from v1. Every infographic must include all three richness layers:

### Layer 1: Background Decorative Geometry

The background must not be a flat plain white or lavender. Add abstract geometric shapes behind the content:

- **Large partial circles**: 1-2 large circles (200-300px radius) partially visible at the image corners, using HelloTalk Purple at **5-8% opacity**. Gives a subtle branded depth without cluttering.
- **Diagonal accent band**: Optional — a very faint diagonal stripe or arc across the lower-right background using `#5856D6` at 4% opacity.
- **Dot grid or subtle pattern**: A very faint dot grid covering the background using `#5856D6` at 3-4% opacity is acceptable for data-heavy charts.

❌ Do NOT use a plain flat white background with zero background elements.
❌ Background elements must be subtle — never compete with foreground content.

### Layer 2: Card / Block Visual Treatment

Every card, block, or row in the infographic must use **filled visual treatment**, not just a colored left border on a white card:

**Option A — Tinted fill card** (preferred for feature/framework cards):
- Card background: accent color at **10-15% opacity** (e.g., for a green-accented card: `rgba(52, 199, 89, 0.12)` background fill)
- Card top header strip: 36-40px colored band at the top of the card using the accent color at **30-40% opacity**
- Small flat line icon (16-20px) inside the header strip on the left

**Option B — Colored header badge card** (preferred for stat/data cards):
- White card background
- Colored rounded badge/pill at the top of the card (full accent color, white label text inside, 6px rounded corners)
- Key data value in large bold text (40-48pt) directly below, in the accent color

**Option C — Filled row** (for bar charts and comparison tables):
- Alternating very light lavender (`#F5F4FA`) and white row backgrounds
- Row labels in colored pill badges on the left
- Data values at the end of each bar in a matching colored rounded container

❌ Do NOT use outline-only boxes (just a colored border with white inside and plain text).
❌ Do NOT use plain colored left borders as the only visual treatment.

### Layer 3: Data Callout Treatment

Key numbers, durations, labels, and values must have visual containers — never just plain colored text:

- **Large numbers** (e.g., "2200h", "24 letters", "4 stages"): Display in a **colored rounded rectangle or pill badge** — white text on full accent color background, or large accent-colored text with a very light accent-tinted background swatch behind it (accent color at 12-15% opacity, 8px rounded corners)
- **Category/section labels** (e.g., "COMPARISON", "MILESTONE TIMELINE"): Must appear as a **filled pill badge** — HelloTalk Purple fill (#5856D6), white text, rounded corners (20px radius), 10-16pt bold
- **Step numbers on timelines**: Must be **filled circles** (36-44px diameter) in full accent colors — not just outlined circles
- **Axis labels on charts**: Wrap in a subtle tinted chip (`#F5F4FA` background, `#1C1C1E` text, 4px radius) rather than bare text

## Icon Elements (v2 addition)

Every card or section block should include a small flat geometric icon to support content meaning:

### Approved icon vocabulary (line icons, single-weight, 18-24px)

| Concept | Icon |
|---|---|
| Speaking / conversation | Simple speech bubble outline |
| Study / learning | Open book outline |
| Time / duration | Clock outline |
| Progress / growth | Upward arrow or stepped bar |
| Grammar / rules | Checkmark or list lines |
| Writing system | Abstract letter shape (A, あ, 한) |
| Native speaker | Person silhouette (minimal, no face detail) |
| Milestone / achievement | Star or flag outline |
| AI / technology | Circuit or chip outline |
| Travel | Location pin outline |

### Icon usage rules

- Place icon in the **card header area**, left-aligned, same color as the accent
- Icon should be the same color as the card's accent, at 80% opacity
- Size: 18-24px equivalent — subtle, not dominant
- Style: single-weight line icon, flat, no fill, no drop shadow
- ❌ No cartoon icons, no emoji-style icons, no illustrated characters

## Brand Presence (v2 upgrade)

The brand marker should be more substantial than 3 tiny dots in the corner:

### Recommended brand treatment

**Option A — Colored left accent strip**:
A 6-8px wide vertical strip along the full left edge of the image in HelloTalk Purple (`#5856D6`). Clean and unobtrusive but clearly branded.

**Option B — Purple header band**:
The title section (top 15-18% of the image) has a very light purple background (`#5856D6` at 8% opacity) as a distinct header zone, with the category pill badge and title sitting inside it.

**Option C — Bottom brand strip**:
A 32-40px tall footer band in HelloTalk Purple with "HelloTalk Blog" in white 12pt text centered — replacing the current faint gray text.

**Use at least one of these options in every infographic.** The 3-dot cluster alone is insufficient.

## Layout Rules (v2)

### Card grid layouts

- **4 items**: Always use **2×2 grid** — never 3+1
- **3 items**: Use **1×3 horizontal row** or **1×3 vertical stack** — never 2+1
- **5+ items**: Use **2 columns** with rows, or a stacked single-column list
- ❌ Never leave an orphaned single card in a bottom row of a multi-column grid

### Spacing and density

- No section of the image should have more than **15% blank/unused space**
- Use decorative geometry (Layer 1) to fill any large empty zones
- Timeline images: the space above the timeline node row should be used for the title header with colored background band; space below nodes is used for milestone cards without excessive padding

### Aspect ratios

- **Hero (article top)**: 16:9 — 1600 × 900 px
- **In-article**: 16:9 or 4:3 — 1200 × 675 / 1200 × 900 px
- **UI screenshot**: 9:16 — 1080 × 1920 px
- **Inline small**: 1:1 — 800 × 800 px

## Lighting & Atmosphere

For images that include any photographic or rendered elements, use these lighting cues:

- **Soft bright studio lighting** — even, well-lit, no harsh shadows
- **Airy atmosphere** — open compositions with breathing room
- **Optimistic mood** — bright but not aggressive, friendly without being saccharine
- **High-key exposure** — leaning toward bright rather than moody

## Photorealistic Website/App Hero Style

This is now the default image direction for generated HelloTalk blog visuals.

### Required elements

- A generated, non-identifiable adult language learner as the main subject. The person should look like a real photograph, but must not resemble a celebrity, public figure, influencer, or real private person.
- Preserve brand feel through purple UI, rounded cards, chat bubbles, and the overall website/app layout.
- A realistic smartphone or app-card mockup showing common language-exchange elements: purple chat bubbles, voice/message chips, correction cards, or short language-practice prompts. Keep UI generic and truthful unless a real screenshot is provided.
- A website hero layout: brand-colored headline area on one side, photoreal learner and phone mockup on the other side, with rounded cards and soft purple geometry connecting them.
- White or very light lavender background, HelloTalk Purple `#5856D6` as the dominant visual accent, and small accents of green, pink, or yellow only where useful.

### Photo treatment

- Bright natural daylight or soft studio lighting
- Optimistic expression, candid but polished
- Shallow depth of field is acceptable, but all app/icon/card elements must stay readable
- Clothing and setting can subtly match the target language/travel context, without stereotypes
- Use commercial website campaign quality, not casual selfie quality

### Text and UI safety

- Keep visible text short: 1 headline, 2-4 small chips/cards, optional chat bubbles
- Do not copy exact text from reference images
- Do not invent unsupported product features, statistics, testimonials, rankings, reviews, or guarantees
- Do not show real user data, phone numbers, emails, precise addresses, or identifiable profiles

## Typography

### Recommended fonts (for infographics)

- **Primary**: Inter, SF Pro Display, system-ui
- **Secondary**: Source Sans Pro, IBM Plex Sans
- **Avoid**: Serif fonts (too formal), display fonts (too decorative), handwritten fonts

### Hierarchy

- **H1 in image**: 32-40pt, bold, `#1C1C1E` (dark text)
- **H2 in image**: 22-28pt, semibold, `#5856D6` (HelloTalk Purple for emphasis)
- **Body in image**: 14-16pt, regular, `#1C1C1E` or `#6E6E73`
- **Captions**: 12-14pt, regular, `#6E6E73`
- **Data callout numbers**: 40-48pt, bold, in accent color with background container

## Photo + UI Overlay Style

### What to ASK FOR

- **Photorealistic generated people** — natural expression, real-photo lighting, non-identifiable adult learner
- **Website/app marketing layout** — brand area, headline zone, phone mockup, rounded cards, purple chat bubbles
- **Geometric UI shapes** — clean circles, rectangles, rounded corners, curved purple footer/edge shapes
- **Compact article overlays** — short chips, small cards, or callouts that explain the article point without turning into a flat-only infographic
- **Generous whitespace** — content should breathe, with soft lavender and subtle geometry filling empty zones
- **Vibrant purple as anchor** — HelloTalk Purple `#5856D6` should always be clearly visible

### What to AVOID

- **Skeuomorphism** — no fake textures, leather, paper
- **3D renders** — no Cinema 4D / Blender aesthetic
- **Generic stock photo aesthetic** — no unrelated "diverse smiling young adults at laptops"; the photo must be article-specific and app-focused
- **Cartoon characters** — no Memoji-style faces, no Pixar-style scenes
- **Hand-drawn doodles** — no quirky illustration style
- **Dark/moody color schemes** — must feel bright and optimistic
- **Cluttered compositions** — no "everything but the kitchen sink"
- **Outline-only card boxes** — no plain borders as the only visual treatment (v2)
- **Plain colored text for data** — key values need pill/badge containers (v2)

---

## Cultural Considerations

When the article references specific languages/cultures, ensure visuals are:

### ✅ Respectful

- Use generated people with natural, non-stereotyped representation (avoid hyper-specific racial coding unless culturally relevant)
- If showing flags or scripts, use them functionally (not decoratively)
- For Asian language articles (Japanese, Korean, Chinese), use actual native scripts when possible

### ❌ Avoid

- Cultural stereotypes (sombreros, anime eyes, kimonos as default)
- Outdated cultural symbols
- Mixing scripts from different language families in confusing ways

This updated style anchor MUST replace the v1 anchor at the bottom of EVERY image prompt:

```
STYLE & ATMOSPHERE: Create a bright photorealistic HelloTalk website/app marketing composite. Main subject is a generated, non-identifiable adult language learner in natural real-photo style, using a smartphone with an optimistic expression. Lighting is soft daylight or bright studio light, airy, polished, and commercial-quality. Background is clean white or very light lavender (#FAFAFE) with subtle HelloTalk Purple (#5856D6) geometry.

BRAND REQUIREMENTS: HelloTalk Purple (#5856D6) must dominate chat bubbles, phone UI highlights, pill badges, rounded cards, and a curved bottom/edge shape. Small accents may use green (#34C759), pink (#FF2D92), and yellow (#FFCC00).

COMPOSITION REQUIREMENTS: Use a website hero layout with brand-colored headline/chips on one side, photoreal learner and realistic phone mockup on the other. Phone mockup may show common language-exchange chat/voice elements in purple and white, but must not invent unsupported product features, fake reviews, exact user data, or unsupported claims. Cards/chips must be filled rounded containers with soft shadows, not outline-only boxes.

AVOID: flat-only infographic output, cartoon characters, 3D render style, generic stock-photo cliches, unrelated logos, competitor UI, dark or moody palettes, excessive text, real public figures or identifiable private people, fake user data, fake metrics/testimonials, or unsupported product claims.
```
